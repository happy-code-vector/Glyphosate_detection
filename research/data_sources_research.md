# Glyphosate & Pesticide Residue Testing Data Sources

## 1. Government Agencies

### United States

#### USDA Pesticide Data Program (PDP)
- **URL**: https://www.ams.usda.gov/datasets/pdp
- **Data Format**: CSV, Excel, PDF reports
- **Coverage**: US food supply, 1991-present (annual)
- **Commodities**: Fresh/processed fruits, vegetables, grains, dairy, meat, water
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes (public download)
- **Notes**: The gold standard for US pesticide monitoring. Tests 10,000+ samples annually. Includes glyphosate testing since ~2011. Raw microdata and summary reports available.

#### FDA Pesticide Residue Monitoring Program
- **URL**: https://www.fda.gov/food/pesticide-residue-monitoring
- **Data Format**: PDF reports, some CSV
- **Coverage**: US domestic and imported foods
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Tests for ~800 pesticides. Began expanded glyphosate testing around 2016-2017. Annual reports available.

#### EPA Tolerance Levels (MRLs)
- **URL**: https://www.epa.gov/pesticide-tolerances
- **Data Format**: CFR (Code of Federal Regulations), searchable database
- **Coverage**: US regulatory limits for all registered pesticides
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Sets maximum legal residue levels (tolerances) for glyphosate on ~200+ crops. Not testing data per se, but the regulatory benchmark.

#### USGS Pesticide National Synthesis Project
- **URL**: https://www.usgs.gov/mission-areas/water-resources/science/pesticide-national-synthesis-project
- **Data Format**: CSV, maps, reports
- **Coverage**: US water resources (surface/groundwater)
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Monitors pesticide occurrence in water, not food. Useful for environmental exposure data.

### European Union

#### EFSA (European Food Safety Authority)
- **URL**: https://www.efsa.europa.eu/en/data-report/pesticide-residues-food
- **Data Format**: PDF reports, Excel data tables
- **Coverage**: EU member states + Iceland, Norway (27+ countries)
- **Years**: Annual reports since 2006
- **Commodities**: Fruits, vegetables, cereals, animal products, processed foods
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: EU-coordinated control programme (EU MACP) + national monitoring. Tests ~100,000+ samples/year. ~95-97% compliance rate. Includes glyphosate data.

#### EU Pesticides Database
- **URL**: https://ec.europa.eu/food/plant/pesticides/eu-pesticides-database/mrls/
- **Data Format**: Searchable web database
- **Coverage**: EU MRLs for all approved pesticides
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Search by pesticide name or crop. Contains regulatory MRLs, not testing results.

#### BfR (German Federal Institute for Risk Assessment)
- **URL**: https://www.bfr.bund.de/en/pesticide_residues_in_food-852.html
- **Data Format**: PDF reports, scientific publications
- **Coverage**: Germany, EU
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Conducts risk assessment for pesticide residues in German food supply.

#### UK Food Standards Agency (FSA)
- **URL**: https://www.food.gov.uk/research/pesticide-residues-in-food
- **Data Format**: PDF reports, data tables
- **Coverage**: UK (post-Brexit independent monitoring)
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes

#### Food Safety Commission of Japan
- **URL**: https://www.fsc.go.jp/english/
- **Data Format**: PDF reports
- **Coverage**: Japan
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes

### Other Countries

#### Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ)
- **URL**: https://www.foodstandards.gov.au/
- **Data Format**: PDF reports
- **Coverage**: Australia, New Zealand
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes

#### Health Canada - Pesticide Residue Monitoring
- **URL**: https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/pesticide-registration.html
- **Data Format**: PDF reports
- **Coverage**: Canada
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes

#### China National Food Safety Standards
- **URL**: http://www.chinafoodsci.com/ (GB 2763 standard)
- **Data Format**: PDF standards (Chinese language)
- **Coverage**: China
- **Freely Accessible**: Limited (standards often behind paywall)

## 2. International Organizations

#### FAO/WHO Codex Alimentarius - Pesticide MRL Database
- **URL**: https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/codex-texts/dbs/pestres/en/
- **Data Format**: Searchable web database
- **Coverage**: Global (international standards)
- **Commodities**: 200+ food commodities
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: THE international reference for MRLs. Search by pesticide or commodity. Derived from JMPR evaluations. ~5,000+ MRL entries.

#### FAO/WHO JMPR (Joint Meeting on Pesticide Residues)
- **URL**: https://www.who.int/foodsafety/areas_work/chemical-risks/jmpr/en/
- **Data Format**: PDF monographs, evaluations, reports
- **Coverage**: Global (international risk assessments)
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Expert body that evaluates toxicology and residue data. Publishes ADIs and ARfDs. Monographs contain detailed residue trial data from field studies worldwide.

#### WHO GEMS/Food (Global Environment Monitoring System - Food Contamination Monitoring)
- **URL**: https://www.who.int/foodsafety/areas_work/food-safety/gems/en/
- **Data Format**: Database, reports
- **Coverage**: 70+ countries
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes (requires registration)
- **Notes**: WHO's global food contamination monitoring network. Covers pesticide residues among other contaminants.

#### IARC (International Agency for Research on Cancer)
- **URL**: https://www.iarc.who.int/
- **Data Format**: PDF monographs
- **Coverage**: Global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Classified glyphosate as "probably carcinogenic to humans" (Group 2A) in 2015. Monograph 112 contains the evaluation.

#### FAO Food Safety Statistics
- **URL**: https://www.fao.org/food/food-safety-quality/en/
- **Data Format**: Reports, data portals
- **Coverage**: Global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes

## 3. Academic & Research Databases

#### PubChem
- **URL**: https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
- **Data Format**: Searchable database, API, downloadable data
- **Coverage**: Chemical properties, bioactivity, toxicity data
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Search for "glyphosate" (CID: 3496) for chemical properties, safety data, bioactivity assays, and linked literature.

#### EPA CompTox Chemicals Dashboard
- **URL**: https://comptox.epa.gov/dashboard/
- **Data Format**: Searchable database, API, downloadable data
- **Coverage**: US, global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Comprehensive toxicology and exposure data for 900,000+ chemicals including glyphosate. Links to hazard, exposure, and risk data.

#### EFSA OpenFoodTox
- **URL**: https://openfoodtox.efsa.europa.eu/
- **Data Format**: Searchable database
- **Coverage**: EU, global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Open toxicological database on substances in food and feed, including pesticides.

#### PPDB (Pesticide Properties Database) - University of Hertfordshire
- **URL**: https://sitem.herts.ac.uk/aeru/ppdb/
- **Data Format**: Searchable database
- **Coverage**: Global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Comprehensive data on chemical/physical properties, environmental fate, ecotoxicology, and regulatory status of ~2,300 pesticides. Search for glyphosate for detailed property data.

#### PubMed / MEDLINE
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
- **Data Format**: Literature database (citations, abstracts, full text links)
- **Coverage**: Global academic research
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Search "glyphosate residue food" for thousands of peer-reviewed studies. Many studies include original testing data.

#### Web of Science / Scopus
- **URL**: https://www.webofscience.com/ / https://www.scopus.com/
- **Data Format**: Literature database
- **Coverage**: Global academic research
- **Freely Accessible**: No (institutional subscription required)
- **Notes**: More comprehensive than PubMed for agricultural/food science literature.

#### ChemSpider (Royal Society of Chemistry)
- **URL**: http://www.chemspider.com/
- **Data Format**: Searchable database
- **Coverage**: Global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Free chemical structure database with linked data sources.

#### The Detox Project
- **URL**: https://www.detoxproject.org/
- **Glyphosate Residue Free Certification**: https://www.detoxproject.org/glyphosate-residue-free/
- **Data Format**: PDF reports, certification database
- **Coverage**: US, global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Runs "Glyphosate Residue Free" certification program. Publishes independent testing results. Tests food, water, and human biomonitoring (urine).

#### Moms Across America
- **URL**: https://www.momsacrossamerica.com/
- **Data Format**: PDF reports
- **Coverage**: US
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Has tested breast milk, wine, school lunches, and other foods for glyphosate. Published results showing widespread contamination.

#### Food Democracy Now!
- **URL**: https://www.fooddemocracynow.org/
- **Data Format**: PDF reports
- **Coverage**: US
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Has conducted glyphosate testing campaigns on popular foods.

#### Health Research Institute (HRI Labs)
- **URL**: https://www.hri-labs.com/
- **Data Format**: Testing services (paid), some published data
- **Coverage**: US
- **Freely Accessible**: Partial (some reports free, testing services paid)
- **Notes**: Independent lab that tests for glyphosate in food and human urine. Works with advocacy groups.

#### Soil Association (UK)
- **URL**: https://www.soilassociation.org/
- **Data Format**: PDF reports
- **Coverage**: UK, Europe
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: UK organic certification body. Publishes research on pesticide residues in food.

## 5. Open Data Portals

#### Data.gov (US)
- **URL**: https://www.data.gov/
- **Search**: "pesticide residue" or "pesticide data program"
- **Data Format**: CSV, JSON, API
- **Coverage**: US
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: US government open data portal. USDA PDP data is available here. ~200+ datasets related to pesticides.

#### EU Open Data Portal
- **URL**: https://data.europa.eu/
- **Search**: "pesticide residues"
- **Data Format**: CSV, JSON, API
- **Coverage**: EU
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: European Commission open data. EFSA monitoring data available.

#### Kaggle
- **URL**: https://www.kaggle.com/
- **Search**: "pesticide residue" or "glyphosate"
- **Data Format**: CSV, notebooks
- **Coverage**: Various (user-uploaded datasets)
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes (registration required)
- **Notes**: Community-uploaded datasets including cleaned USDA PDP data, food safety datasets, and analytical notebooks.

#### Our World in Data
- **URL**: https://ourworldindata.org/
- **Data Format**: Charts, CSV download, GitHub
- **Coverage**: Global
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: Environmental and health data visualizations. Some pesticide-related data available.

#### FAOSTAT
- **URL**: https://www.fao.org/faostat/en/
- **Data Format**: CSV, API, bulk download
- **Coverage**: 200+ countries, 1961-present
- **Freely Accessible**: Yes
- **Notes**: FAO's main statistical database. Includes pesticide use data by country and crop (not residue testing, but usage data).

## 7. Recommended Data Acquisition Strategy

### For US glyphosate residue data:
1. **Primary**: USDA PDP annual data (CSV download) - most comprehensive, longest time series
2. **Secondary**: FDA monitoring reports
3. **Supplementary**: EWG Food Scores for consumer-facing data

### For EU glyphosate residue data:
1. **Primary**: EFSA annual monitoring reports (Excel data tables)
2. **Secondary**: Individual member state reports (BfR for Germany, FSA for UK)

### For international/global MRL reference:
1. **Primary**: Codex Alimentarius MRL database
2. **Secondary**: JMPR monographs for detailed residue trial data

### For academic research context:
1. PubMed systematic reviews
2. PPDB for chemical property data
3. EPA CompTox for toxicology data

### For consumer/advocacy testing:
1. EWG reports and Food Scores
2. Detox Project certification and testing data

## 8. Data Quality Notes

- **Government sources** (USDA, EFSA) are the most rigorous: standardized methods, large sample sizes, representative sampling, validated analytical methods (LC-MS/MS)
- **NGO sources** (EWG, Detox Project) use accredited labs but may have smaller sample sizes and different health benchmarks than regulators
- **Codex/JMPR** data represents international consensus but focuses on MRLs rather than monitoring data
- **Academic studies** vary widely in methodology; prefer peer-reviewed publications with validated methods
- **Kaggle datasets** should be verified against original government sources before use
